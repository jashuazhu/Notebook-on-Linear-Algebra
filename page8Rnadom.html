Random Matrices

Wishart covariance models, Marchenko–Pastur and Wigner laws — with usage and illustrative calculations.

1) Wishart Distribution & Mean

Background & usage. If $x_i\\sim\\mathcal N(0,\\Sigma)$ i.i.d., the sample covariance $S=\\sum x_i x_i^\top$ is Wishart. Used in multivariate statistics and ML covariance estimation.

If \(x_i\\sim\\mathcal N(0,\Sigma)\) and \(S=\sum_{i=1}^n x_i x_i^\top\), then \(S\\sim W_p(n,\Sigma)\) and \[ \mathbb E[S]=n\,\Sigma. \]
Proof \(\mathbb E[x_i x_i^\top]=\Sigma\) by definition of covariance; linearity gives \(\mathbb E[S]=\sum \mathbb E[x_i x_i^\top]=n\Sigma\).
Example: \(\Sigma=\begin{bmatrix}2&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\), \(n=3\) ⇒ \(\mathbb E[S]=\operatorname{diag}(6,3)\).

2) Marchenko–Pastur (MP) Law (Statement)

Background & usage. Describes limiting eigenvalue distribution of sample covariance when $p,n\\to\\infty$ with $p/n\\to\\gamma$; crucial in high-dimensional PCA.

For \(X\\in\\mathbb{R}^{p\\times n}\) with i.i.d. entries of variance 1, eigenvalues of \(\tfrac{1}{n}XX^\top\) converge in distribution to MP with density on \[ \big[(1-\sqrt{\gamma})^2,\ (1+\sqrt{\gamma})^2\big],\qquad \gamma=\lim p/n. \]
Proof sketch Use Stieltjes transform and moment method: match moments of \(\tfrac{1}{n}XX^\top\) with Catalan numbers scaled by \(\gamma\); apply analytic inversion to obtain MP density and support.
Example: \(\gamma=1/4\Rightarrow (1\pm \tfrac12)^2\in[0.25,\ 2.25]\). In practice, eigenvalues of \(\tfrac{1}{n}XX^\top\) lie mostly in this interval for large \(n\).

3) Wigner Semicircle Law (Statement)

Background & usage. Governs eigenvalues of large symmetric matrices with i.i.d. centered entries; appears in physics and combinatorics.

For symmetric \(W_n\) with i.i.d. entries (mean 0, variance \(1/n\)), the empirical eigenvalue distribution converges to density \[ \rho(x)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{4-x^2}\ \ \text{on }[-2,2]. \]
Proof sketch Moment method: \(\mathbb E[\tfrac1n\operatorname{tr}(W_n^k)]\) counts non-crossing pairings; normalized moments converge to Catalan numbers, whose generating function yields the semicircle density.
Example: Take \(W=\begin{bmatrix}0&1&-1\\1&0&1\\-1&1&0\end{bmatrix}/\sqrt{3}\). Numerically its eigenvalues lie within \([-2,2]\) and cluster near the semicircle shape as \(n\) grows.

4) Concentration of Sample Covariance (Heuristic Bound)

Background & usage. Shows deviation of empirical covariance from true covariance in operator norm; informs sample complexity for PCA/estimation.

For sub-Gaussian $x_i\\in\\mathbb{R}^p$ with covariance $\Sigma$, with high probability \[ \left\| \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n x_i x_i^\top - \Sigma \right\|_2 \lesssim \|\Sigma\|_2\left(\sqrt{\frac{p}{n}}+\frac{p}{n}\right). \]
Proof sketch Use matrix Bernstein (or Vershynin’s sub-Gaussian tail bounds) on centered matrices \(x_i x_i^\top-\Sigma\) and take a union bound over an \(\epsilon\)-net on the sphere.
Example: \(p=50,n=200,\ \|\Sigma\|_2=3\Rightarrow\) RHS \(\approx 3(\sqrt{0.25}+0.25)=3(0.5+0.25)=2.25\).
