Random Matrices

Wishart covariance models, Marchenko–Pastur and Wigner laws, plus concentration heuristics.

1) Wishart Distribution and Mean

Background & usage. If $x_i \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \Sigma)$ independently, the sample covariance $S = \sum x_i x_i^\top$ is Wishart. Used in multivariate statistics and covariance estimation.

If $x_i \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \Sigma)$ and $S = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i x_i^\top$, then $S \sim W_p(n, \Sigma)$ with expectation \[ \mathbb{E}[S] = n \Sigma. \]
Proof Since $\mathbb{E}[x_i x_i^\top] = \Sigma$ by the definition of covariance, linearity gives $\mathbb{E}[S] = \sum \mathbb{E}[x_i x_i^\top] = n \Sigma$.
Example: With $\Sigma = \begin{bmatrix}2 & 0\\ 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}$ and $n = 3$, the mean is $\operatorname{diag}(6, 3)$.

2) Marchenko–Pastur Law (Statement)

Background & usage. Describes limiting eigenvalue distribution of sample covariance when $p, n \to \infty$ with $p/n \to \gamma$; crucial in high-dimensional PCA.

For $X \in \mathbb{R}^{p \times n}$ with independent entries of variance $1$, eigenvalues of $\tfrac{1}{n} X X^\top$ converge in distribution to Marchenko–Pastur with support \[ \big[(1 - \sqrt{\gamma})^2,\ (1 + \sqrt{\gamma})^2\big], \qquad \gamma = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{p}{n}. \]
Proof sketch Use the Stieltjes transform and moment method: match moments of $\tfrac{1}{n} X X^\top$ with Catalan numbers scaled by $\gamma$, then invert analytically to obtain the density and support.
Example: If $\gamma = 1/4$, the support is $[(1 - \tfrac{1}{2})^2, (1 + \tfrac{1}{2})^2] = [0.25, 2.25]$. Eigenvalues of $\tfrac{1}{n} X X^\top$ concentrate in this interval for large $n$.

3) Wigner Semicircle Law (Statement)

Background & usage. Governs eigenvalues of large symmetric matrices with centered independent entries; appears in physics and combinatorics.

For symmetric $W_n$ with independent entries (mean $0$, variance $1/n$), the empirical eigenvalue distribution converges to \[ \rho(x) = \frac{1}{2 \pi} \sqrt{4 - x^2} \quad \text{on } [-2, 2]. \]
Proof sketch The moment method counts non-crossing pairings: $\mathbb{E}\big[\tfrac{1}{n} \operatorname{tr}(W_n^k)\big]$ converges to Catalan numbers, whose generating function yields the semicircle density.
Example: For $W = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & -1\\ 1 & 0 & 1\\ -1 & 1 & 0\end{bmatrix}$, eigenvalues lie within $[-2, 2]$ and approximate the semicircle shape as $n$ grows.

4) Concentration of Sample Covariance (Heuristic)

Background & usage. Bounds deviation of empirical covariance from the true covariance in operator norm; informs sample complexity for PCA.

For sub-Gaussian $x_i \in \mathbb{R}^p$ with covariance $\Sigma$, with high probability \[ \left\| \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n x_i x_i^\top - \Sigma \right\|_2 \lesssim \|\Sigma\|_2 \left( \sqrt{\frac{p}{n}} + \frac{p}{n} \right). \]
Proof sketch Apply matrix Bernstein (or Vershynin's sub-Gaussian tail bounds) to centered matrices $x_i x_i^\top - \Sigma$ and take a union bound over an $\varepsilon$-net on the unit sphere.
Example: If $p = 50$, $n = 200$, and $\|\Sigma\|_2 = 3$, the bound gives approximately $3 (\sqrt{0.25} + 0.25) = 2.25$.
